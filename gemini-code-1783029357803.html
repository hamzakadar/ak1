The Silver Lead Project

Protecting Vulnerable Adults and Taxpayer Dollars

What The Silver Lead Project Does

The Silver Lead Project reviews community-submitted concerns involving vulnerable adults, licensed care providers, fraud, neglect, exploitation, unsafe practices, billing concerns, retaliation, and misuse of taxpayer-funded care programs.

The project receives leads from people close to the care system, including clients, families, staff, former staff, advocates, whistleblowers, and community members.

When a concern is submitted, the project reviews the issue, gathers additional information when needed, works with the person who submitted the lead, organizes supporting documents, identifies patterns, and works toward a reasoned conclusion. When concerns appear credible and actionable, information may be referred to appropriate oversight, licensing, investigative, prosecutorial, media, or public accountability channels.

Leads Come From the Community

People closest to the care system often see warning signs first. Leads come from:

  • Clients receiving services & Their Families
  • Staff members & Former staff members
  • Patient and systemic Advocates
  • Whistleblowers with inside knowledge
  • Concerned citizens and community members

If someone witnessed unsafe care, noticed a pattern, reviewed concerning records, experienced retaliation, or identified possible fraud or misuse of public funds, their information may help protect vulnerable adults and support accountability.

Concerns We Review

  • Vulnerable adult abuse & neglect
  • Financial exploitation & provider misconduct
  • Unsafe care practices & staffing concerns affecting safety
  • Billing concerns, fraud, waste, or abuse of public funds
  • Retaliation & falsified records/billing
  • Failure to provide services & suspicious claims
  • Licensing concerns & improper ownership/management practices

How the Process Works

Step 1: Submit a Lead - A community member or whistleblower submits a concern.

Step 2: Initial Review - The project reviews the concern to understand the provider involved and potential risks.

Step 3: Research and Documentation - The project gathers information, reviews records, and identifies patterns.

Step 4: Provider Contact When Appropriate - When enough credible concern exists, and when safe and appropriate, the provider may be notified and given a choice: enter independent functional oversight with AnchorPoint Health Group or have the matter proceed to appropriate accountability channels.

Step 5: Referral or Escalation - Serious concerns are referred to appropriate oversight, licensing, investigative, prosecutorial, media, or public accountability channels.

A Careful Step Before Escalation: In certain cases, if the provider may still be stabilized through structured oversight, the provider may be given an opportunity to enter independent functional oversight with AnchorPoint Health Group before further escalation. If the provider declines, does not respond, or fails to cooperate, the matter may proceed to appropriate accountability channels.

Our Approach & Impact

Accountability must be strong, but careful. Serious concerns should not be ignored, but providers should not be destroyed based on rumors or incomplete info. The goal is to protect vulnerable adults, safeguard taxpayer dollars, respect due process, and hold bad actors accountable.

Documentation submitted through this work may contribute to accountability outcomes involving provider scrutiny, stronger oversight attention, corrective action, provider closures, owner exclusions, or further review by government agencies, courts, regulators, prosecutors, licensing bodies, media, or other third parties.
