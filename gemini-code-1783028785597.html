Protecting Vulnerable Adults and Taxpayer Dollars

The Silver Lead Project reviews community-submitted concerns involving vulnerable adults, licensed care providers, fraud, neglect, exploitation, unsafe practices, billing concerns, retaliation, and misuse of taxpayer-funded care programs.

Community leads. Evidence built. Accountability delivered.

What The Silver Lead Project Does

The Silver Lead Project receives leads from people close to the care system, including clients, families, staff, former staff, advocates, whistleblowers, and community members.

When a concern is submitted, the project reviews the issue, gathers additional information when needed, works with the person who submitted the lead, organizes supporting documents, identifies patterns, and works toward a reasoned conclusion.

When concerns appear credible and actionable, The Silver Lead Project may refer organized information to appropriate oversight, licensing, investigative, prosecutorial, media, or public accountability channels.

Leads Come From the Community

Clients

Individuals actively or previously receiving long-term support and care services.

Families

Relatives and legal guardians observing direct operational conditions.
