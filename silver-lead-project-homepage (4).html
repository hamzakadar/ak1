Overview

What The Silver Lead Project Does

The Silver Lead Project receives leads from people close to the care system, including clients, families, staff, former staff, advocates, whistleblowers, and community members.

When a concern is submitted, the project reviews the issue, gathers additional information when needed, works with the person who submitted the lead, organizes supporting documents, identifies patterns, and works toward a reasoned conclusion.

When appropriate: when concerns appear serious and credible, The Silver Lead Project may provide a limited opportunity for the provider to submit independent review findings from a qualified company before a final referral decision is made.
Where Leads Come From

Leads Come From the Community

People closest to the care system often see warning signs first. If someone witnessed unsafe care, noticed a pattern, reviewed concerning records, experienced retaliation, or identified possible fraud or misuse of public funds, their information may help protect vulnerable adults and support accountability.

Process

How the Process Works

1

Call to Report a Concern

A client, family member, staff member, former staff member, advocate, whistleblower, or community member calls (763) 306-0921 to report a concern.

2

Initial Review

The Silver Lead Project reviews the concern to understand the provider involved, the possible risk to vulnerable adults, and whether taxpayer-funded services may be involved.

3

Research and Documentation

The project may gather more information, review available documents, organize records, create timelines, and identify patterns.

4

Limited Provider Notice When Appropriate

When enough credible concern exists, and when safe and appropriate, the provider may receive formal notice and a limited opportunity to submit independent review findings from a qualified company before a final referral decision is made.

5

Independent Review Findings Only

The Silver Lead Project considers independent review findings from qualified companies as provider response materials.

6

Final Review Decision

After reviewing the available information and any independent review findings submitted, The Silver Lead Project determines whether to close the matter, continue review, request more information, recognize remediation, or refer credible unresolved concerns to appropriate accountability channels.

For Providers

Provider Response Process

The Silver Lead Project believes serious concerns should be reviewed carefully. When appropriate and safe, a provider may be given a limited opportunity to submit independent review findings before a final referral decision is made.

Only independent review findings from qualified companies are considered as provider response materials.

Independent review findings may address

Compliance concerns Billing concerns Documentation concerns Financial controls Staff accountability Operational systems Corrective action Functional oversight needs Provider stabilization Future-risk prevention
This process is a review opportunity, not a legal proceeding. Providers retain the right to also report to, or seek guidance from, their own legal counsel and applicable regulatory bodies at any time.
Verification

Independent Review Findings

In some cases, serious remediation requires more than a written explanation. The Silver Lead Project considers independent review findings from qualified companies that can assess whether the provider has corrected or is actively correcting the concerns identified.

Independent review may involve

Compliance review Billing review Documentation review Financial controls review Operational review Staff accountability review Care delivery systems review Internal policy review Functional oversight review Provider stabilization review

The Silver Lead Project may identify qualified independent review company options. Providers may also propose another qualified company for consideration.

Options

Qualified Review Company Options

When independent review is appropriate, The Silver Lead Project may provide a list of qualified companies that can perform review or remediation-related work. These companies may include organizations with experience in:

Healthcare operations
Provider compliance
Billing and revenue systems
Documentation review
Staff accountability
Financial controls
Corrective action verification
Functional oversight
Provider stabilization

Providers may choose from the listed qualified companies or propose another qualified company for consideration.

Disclosure: The Silver Lead Project receives no fee, commission, or referral compensation from any independent review company. Providers are free to select any qualified company of their choosing, subject to review of qualifications.
Our Approach

Accountability With Care

The Silver Lead Project believes accountability must be strong, but careful. Serious concerns should not be ignored. At the same time, providers should not be judged based on rumors, incomplete information, or unsupported claims.

When appropriate and safe, the project reviews available information, works with the referring person, identifies patterns, gives the provider a limited opportunity to submit independent review findings, and determines whether further action is warranted.

  • Protect vulnerable adults
  • Safeguard taxpayer dollars
  • Respect due process
  • Preserve needed services when possible
  • Hold bad actors accountable when concerns are serious, credible, and unresolved

About

About The Silver Lead Project

The mission of The Silver Lead Project is to protect vulnerable adults and taxpayer-funded care programs by turning community concerns into researched, organized, evidence-supported accountability review and referral decisions.

The Silver Lead Project is a Minnesota-rooted, community-powered program. We are not a government agency, law enforcement body, or licensing authority — we organize evidence and, when warranted, refer credible unresolved concerns to the accountability channels equipped to act on them.
